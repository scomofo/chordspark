# ChordSpark Curriculum Addendum: Gap Fills

## 1. Strumming Pattern Progression

Strumming develops on its own parallel track alongside chord learning. Each pattern is introduced when the learner has enough chord fluency to handle the cognitive split.

### Strumming Levels

**S1: All Downstrokes (Sessions 1вҖ“7)**
Pattern: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ (one strum per beat)
BPM: 60вҖ“70
When: Introduced with Level 1 chords (Em, Em7)
Why first: Simplest motor pattern. Arm moves in one direction. Zero syncopation. Learner can focus 90% on chord shapes, 10% on rhythm.

Songs that work with all downstrokes:
- "Horse With No Name" (Em + D6/9)
- "Three Little Birds" (A, D, E) вҖ” reggae feel is naturally downstroke-heavy
- "Hound Dog" (A, D, E)

**S2: Down-Down-Up (Sessions 8вҖ“12)**
Pattern: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ‘ (three strums per bar, waltz-like feel)
BPM: 65вҖ“75
When: Introduced with Level 3 chords (A, D)
Why now: Adds the first upstroke. Only one upstroke per bar keeps it manageable. Learner has 3+ chords and basic transitions are becoming automatic.

Songs that work:
- "Amazing Grace" (3/4 time)
- Simplified "Leaving on a Jet Plane"

**S3: The Campfire Strum (Sessions 13вҖ“18)**
Pattern: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ‘ вҶ‘ вҶ“ вҶ‘ (the universal beginner pattern)
BPM: 70вҖ“85
When: Introduced mid-Level 3 to Level 4 (A, D, E, G, C)
Why now: This is THE pattern for 80% of campfire songs. By this point, chord changes are smooth enough that the learner can split attention between strumming and fretting.

Counting: 1 вҶ“ 2 вҶ“ (3) вҶ‘ вҶ‘ 4 вҶ“ вҶ‘
The "miss" on beat 3's downstroke is what gives it swing.

Songs that work:
- "Knockin' on Heaven's Door"
- "Redemption Song"
- "I'm Yours"
- "Riptide"

**S4: Palm Muting & Percussive Strum (Sessions 19вҖ“24)**
Pattern: вҶ“ x вҶ“ вҶ‘ x вҶ‘ (x = muted "chuck" hit)
BPM: 75вҖ“90
When: Introduced with Level 4вҖ“5 chords
Why now: Adds percussive element. ADHD learners often LOVE this вҖ” it's rhythmic, physical, and sounds "pro" immediately. The muted chuck provides a drum-like hit that satisfies the need for tactile feedback.

How to teach: Rest the fleshy edge of the strumming hand on the strings while strumming. The strings "thunk" instead of ring.

Songs that work:
- "Three Little Birds" (authentic reggae strum)
- "Hey Ya" (simplified)

**S5: Syncopated Strum (Sessions 25вҖ“32)**
Pattern: вҶ“ вҶ‘ (miss) вҶ‘ вҶ“ вҶ‘
BPM: 80вҖ“100
When: Level 5вҖ“6 chords (seventh chords, Dm, Cadd9)
Why now: True syncopation. The "miss" on beat 2's downstroke puts emphasis on the "and" of beat 2. This is the pattern that makes rhythm guitar sound like rhythm guitar.

Songs that work:
- "Wonderwall" (approximation)
- "Good Riddance (Time of Your Life)"

**S6: Fingerpicking Preview (Sessions 33вҖ“40)**
Pattern: Thumb plucks bass, fingers pluck treble strings in pattern (T-1-2-3-2-1)
BPM: 60вҖ“75 (slower because fingers are independent)
When: Level 6вҖ“7 chords
Why now: Provides a radical change of texture. ADHD novelty hit. After 30+ sessions of strumming, fingerpicking feels like learning a whole new instrument.

Songs that work:
- "Dust in the Wind" (simplified)
- "Nothing Else Matters" (intro)
- "Blackbird" (simplified)

**S7: Genre-Specific Patterns (Sessions 41+)**
Introduce style-specific rhythms:
- Blues shuffle: вҶ“ вҶ‘ (swing feel, triplet-based)
- Funk chop: x вҶ“ x вҶ“ вҶ‘ x вҶ‘ (heavy muting)
- Folk Travis picking: alternating bass + melody
- Rock power strum: all downstrokes, heavy, with palm mutes between

### Strumming Integration Rules
- Never introduce a new strum pattern AND a new chord in the same session
- New strum patterns are practiced on the learner's most comfortable 2-chord pair first
- Each pattern gets a minimum of 3 sessions before advancing
- Learner can always "drop back" to a simpler pattern when learning new chords

## 2. Session-by-Session Lesson Plans

### Level 1: First Spark (Sessions 1вҖ“3)

**Session 1: "Hello, Guitar"**
- Spark (1 min): Listen to "Horse With No Name" clip
- New Move (5 min): Learn Em chord. Finger placement. Strum all 6 strings. Repeat 10x.
- Song Slice (5 min): Play Em as a drone under "Horse With No Name" verse (just Em, nothing else)
- Victory Lap (1 min): Strum Em one clean time. Record it.
- Strum: All downstrokes, 60 BPM
- LH: Em only
- RH strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“

**Session 2: "One Less Finger"**
- Spark (1 min): Listen to same clip, now notice how the chord changes
- Review (2 min): Play Em 4x clean
- New Move (4 min): Learn Em7 by lifting ring finger from Em. That's it. One finger less = new chord.
- Song Slice (4 min): Alternate Em вҶ’ Em7 вҶ’ Em вҶ’ Em7 every 4 beats. This IS the "Horse With No Name" pattern.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Play the Em вҶ’ Em7 cycle. You just played your first song.
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ at 60 BPM

**Session 3: "Smooth Moves"**
- Spark (1 min): Full "Horse With No Name" chorus
- Review (2 min): Em вҶ’ Em7 cycle at 60 BPM
- New Move (4 min): Speed up the transition. Target: change chords every 2 beats instead of 4. Practice the lift-place motion until it's automatic.
- Song Slice (4 min): Play along with "Horse With No Name" at 0.75x speed on a streaming platform
- Victory Lap (1 min): Play the cycle at full speed. Record.
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ at 65 BPM

### Level 2: The Anchor (Sessions 4вҖ“7)

**Session 4: "Add a Finger"**
- Spark (1 min): Listen to "Achy Breaky Heart" clip
- Review (2 min): Em вҶ’ Em7 cycle
- New Move (4 min): Learn E major. Start from Em position. Add index finger on 1st fret, G string. Middle and ring stay EXACTLY where they are. Strum all 6 strings.
- Song Slice (4 min): Alternate Em вҶ’ E вҶ’ Em вҶ’ E every 4 beats
- Victory Lap (1 min): Clean E major chord, record
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ at 65 BPM

**Session 5: "Shape Shift"**
- Spark (1 min): Listen to clip of "Fallin'" by Alicia Keys
- Review (2 min): Em вҶ’ E transitions
- New Move (4 min): Learn Am. Show that Am is the SAME SHAPE as E major, moved one string set toward the floor. Practice the "slide" from E вҶ’ Am.
- Song Slice (4 min): E вҶ’ Am вҶ’ E вҶ’ Am cycle. Bass note changes from open low E to open A string.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Em вҶ’ E вҶ’ Am chain. Three chords!
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ at 65 BPM

**Session 6: "The Triangle"**
- Spark (1 min): Preview a song using all three chords
- Review (2 min): E вҶ’ Am transitions
- New Move (4 min): Practice the full triangle: Em вҶ’ E вҶ’ Am вҶ’ E вҶ’ Em. Focus on the anchor finger (middle finger stays on 2nd fret for EmвҶ”E).
- Song Slice (4 min): Simplified "Fallin'" вҖ” Em and Am, back and forth with dramatic strumming
- Victory Lap (1 min): Full triangle, clean
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ at 70 BPM

**Session 7: "Speed Round"**
- Spark (1 min): "Eleanor Rigby" clip
- Review (2 min): Full triangle at 70 BPM
- New Move (4 min): Chord Flash mini-game. Random chord name (Em, E, Am) appears, play it within 3 seconds. Repeat 20x.
- Song Slice (4 min): Simplified "Eleanor Rigby" вҖ” Em and C preview (just use Em for now, feel the rhythm)
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record your fastest clean triangle cycle
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ at 70 BPM

### Level 3: The Power Trio (Sessions 8вҖ“14)

**Session 8: "The A Chord"**
- Spark (1 min): "Three Little Birds" clip вҖ” bright, happy, A-D-E
- Review (2 min): Em вҶ’ E вҶ’ Am triangle
- New Move (4 min): Learn A major. Three fingers lined up on 2nd fret (strings 2, 3, 4). Compact shape. Strum from 5th string down.
- Song Slice (4 min): A вҶ’ E вҶ’ A вҶ’ E cycle (two-chord jam)
- Victory Lap (1 min): Clean A chord
- Strum: Introduce вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ‘ pattern at 65 BPM (S2 strumming level)

**Session 9: "The D Chord"**
- Spark (1 min): "Sweet Caroline" clip
- Review (2 min): A вҶ’ E transitions
- New Move (4 min): Learn D major. Triangle shape on top 3 strings. Only strum top 4 strings (muting lesson: thumb touches 5th/6th strings). Index finger anchor on 2nd fret G string shared with A chord.
- Song Slice (4 min): A вҶ’ D вҶ’ A вҶ’ D cycle. Feel the index finger anchor.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Clean D chord
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ‘ at 65 BPM

**Session 10: "Cowboy Chords Unite"**
- Spark (1 min): "Bad Moon Rising" clip
- Review (2 min): A вҶ’ D transitions
- New Move (4 min): Chain all three: A вҶ’ D вҶ’ E вҶ’ A. This is the I-IV-V in A. Practice the full cycle slowly.
- Song Slice (4 min): "Three Little Birds" вҖ” A, D, E. Play along at 0.8x speed.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Full A вҶ’ D вҶ’ E вҶ’ A cycle
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ‘ at 70 BPM

**Session 11: "Blues Day"**
- Spark (1 min): "Hound Dog" clip
- Review (2 min): A вҶ’ D вҶ’ E cycle
- New Move (4 min): Learn the 12-bar blues pattern: A(4 bars) вҶ’ D(2 bars) вҶ’ A(2 bars) вҶ’ E(1 bar) вҶ’ D(1 bar) вҶ’ A(2 bars). Count bars out loud.
- Song Slice (4 min): Play the 12-bar blues with backing track
- Victory Lap (1 min): One full 12-bar cycle
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ‘ at 70 BPM

**Session 12: "Sweet Caroline"**
- Spark (1 min): Full chorus of "Sweet Caroline"
- Review (2 min): 12-bar blues
- New Move (4 min): Learn the specific chord pattern for "Sweet Caroline" (A вҶ’ D вҶ’ E вҶ’ A). Practice the timing of changes.
- Song Slice (4 min): Play along with the recording at 0.85x speed
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record the chorus
- Strum: Introduce campfire strum вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ‘ вҶ‘ вҶ“ вҶ‘ at 70 BPM (S3)

**Session 13: "Mixing Old and New"**
- Spark (1 min): "Stir It Up" clip
- Review (2 min): Campfire strum on A chord
- New Move (4 min): Practice campfire strum across A вҶ’ D вҶ’ E transitions. Strum doesn't stop for chord changes вҖ” arm keeps moving, even if a beat is missed during the change.
- Song Slice (4 min): "Stir It Up" or "Three Little Birds" with campfire strum
- Victory Lap (1 min): 60-second jam, campfire strum, A вҶ’ D вҶ’ E
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ‘ вҶ‘ вҶ“ вҶ‘ at 75 BPM

**Session 14: "Level 3 Boss"**
- Spark (1 min): Medley of all Level 3 songs
- Review (2 min): Random chord drill вҖ” Em, E, Am, A, D, any order
- New Move (4 min): Full chord vocabulary test. Chord Roulette: random chord name every 4 beats. Include ALL chords learned so far.
- Song Slice (4 min): Pick ANY song from the unlocked list. Learner's choice. (Choice = autonomy = ADHD motivation)
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record your best song
- Strum: Campfire at 80 BPM

### Level 4: Open Road (Sessions 15вҖ“22)

**Session 15: "The G Chord"**
- Spark (1 min): "Love Me Do" clip
- Review (2 min): Campfire strum on A вҶ’ D вҶ’ E
- New Move (4 min): Learn easy G (ring finger 3rd fret low E, middle finger 2nd fret A string). Just two fingers. Strum all 6 strings. Full, resonant sound.
- Song Slice (4 min): G вҶ’ D вҶ’ G вҶ’ D cycle. New territory!
- Victory Lap (1 min): Clean G chord
- Strum: Campfire at 75 BPM (slower for new chord)

**Session 16: "The C Chord"**
- Spark (1 min): "Leaving on a Jet Plane" clip
- Review (2 min): G вҶ’ D transitions
- New Move (4 min): Learn C major. Ring finger 3rd fret A string (anchor from G!), middle 2nd fret D, index 1st fret B. Strum from 5th string. This is the "hardest" common chord вҖ” give it extra time.
- Song Slice (4 min): G вҶ’ C вҶ’ G вҶ’ C. Focus on ring finger anchor.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Clean C chord
- Strum: вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ вҶ“ at 65 BPM (drop back to simple strum for new chord)

**Session 17: "G-C-D: The Other Power Trio"**
- Spark (1 min): "Sweet Home Alabama" clip (yes, it's D-C-G but close enough)
- Review (2 min): G вҶ’ C transitions
- New Move (4 min): G вҶ’ C вҶ’ D chain. Practice each transition: GвҶ’C (ring anchor), CвҶ’D (index near-anchor), DвҶ’G (no anchor вҖ” practice this one extra).
- Song Slice (4 min): "Love Me Do" вҖ” G, C, D. Play along at 0.8x speed.
- Victory Lap (1 min): G вҶ’ C вҶ’ D вҶ’ G clean cycle
- Strum: Campfire at 70 BPM

**Session 18: "The Four Chord Song"**
- Spark (1 min): "Knockin' on Heaven's Door" clip
- Review (2 min): G вҶ’ C вҶ’ D chain
- New Move (4 min): Learn the G вҶ’ D вҶ’ Em вҶ’ C progression (I-V-vi-IV). This is the "axis of awesome" вҖ” the progression that powers hundreds of songs. Practice with Am standing in where needed.
- Song Slice (4 min): "Knockin' on Heaven's Door" вҖ” G, D, Am, C. Play along.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Full progression loop
- Strum: Campfire at 75 BPM

**Session 19: "I'm Yours"**
- Spark (1 min): "I'm Yours" by Jason Mraz
- Review (2 min): G вҶ’ D вҶ’ Em вҶ’ C progression
- New Move (4 min): This song uses the same four chords! Specific timing: G(2 bars) вҶ’ D(2 bars) вҶ’ Em(2 bars) вҶ’ C(2 bars). Relaxed tempo.
- Song Slice (4 min): Play along with "I'm Yours" at 0.85x speed
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record the verse
- Strum: Campfire at 80 BPM

**Session 20: "Stand By Me"**
- Spark (1 min): "Stand By Me" clip
- Review (2 min): "I'm Yours" progression
- New Move (4 min): Slightly different order: G вҶ’ Em вҶ’ C вҶ’ D. Same chords, different emotion. Practice the reordering.
- Song Slice (4 min): "Stand By Me" play-along
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record
- Strum: Campfire at 80 BPM

**Session 21: "Redemption Song"**
- Spark (1 min): "Redemption Song" clip
- Review (2 min): G вҶ’ Em вҶ’ C вҶ’ D
- New Move (4 min): This song uses G, Em, C, Am, D вҖ” ALL chords the learner knows! Practice the specific sequence.
- Song Slice (4 min): "Redemption Song" play-along. Slower tempo, more emotional.
- Victory Lap (1 min): Record
- Strum: Introduce percussive strum (S4) вҶ“ x вҶ“ вҶ‘ x вҶ‘ at 75 BPM

**Session 22: "Level 4 Boss вҖ” Song Buffet"**
- Spark (1 min): Medley of all Level 4 songs
- Review (2 min): Chord Roulette with ALL 8 chords (Em, E, Am, A, D, G, C, Em7)
- New Move (4 min): Transition trainer вҖ” random pairs, metronome at 80 BPM. How many clean transitions in 60 seconds?

## 3. Tempo/BPM Progression

### Master BPM Schedule

| Sessions | Base BPM | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| 1вҖ“3 | 60 | Initial chord forming. Speed irrelevant. |
| 4вҖ“7 | 65 | First transitions. Slow enough to think. |
| 8вҖ“12 | 70 | Power trio. Building muscle memory. |
| 13вҖ“14 | 75вҖ“80 | Campfire strum introduced. Moderate tempo. |
| 15вҖ“18 | 65вҖ“75 | New chords (G, C) вҖ” drop BPM for new shapes, ramp back up. |
| 19вҖ“22 | 75вҖ“85 | Chord vocabulary is solid. Push tempo. |
| 23вҖ“28 | 70вҖ“80 | New key territory (7th chords). Moderate. |
| 29вҖ“34 | 75вҖ“85 | Minor territory and flavor chords. |
| 35вҖ“42 | 65вҖ“85 | F barre chord. Start SLOW (65), build to 85. |
| 43вҖ“50 | 80вҖ“100 | Graduation. Performance-ready tempos. |

### BPM Rules
- When introducing a new chord: drop BPM by 15 from current comfort level
- When introducing a new strum pattern: drop BPM by 10
- Never introduce a new chord AND increase BPM in the same session
- Metronome is ALWAYS visible/audible (ADHD time-blindness aid)
- Learner can override BPM at any time (autonomy)
- "Speed Challenge" mode: play at current BPM + 10 for 30 seconds. Bonus XP.

## 5. Capo Introduction

### When: Session 20 (mid-Level 4)

By session 20, the learner has G, C, D, Em, Am вҖ” enough chords to benefit massively from a capo.

### How to Introduce:

**Session 20 alternate (or bonus session):**

"You already know 5 chord shapes: G, C, D, Em, Am. With a capo, those same shapes can play songs in ANY key. The capo is like a permanent barre across all strings."

**Quick lesson:**
- Capo on fret 1: G shape sounds like Ab. C shape sounds like Db. (Don't explain the theory вҖ” just say "it raises everything by one step.")
- Capo on fret 2: "Free Fallin'" вҖ” play D, G shapes. Sounds like E, A.
- Capo on fret 3: "Here Comes the Sun" вҖ” play G, C, D shapes.
- Capo on fret 4: "Wonderwall" вҖ” play Em, G, D, A shapes.

**The pitch:**
"You just went from playing songs in 2 keys to playing songs in ALL 12 keys. Same 5 shapes. One little clamp."

## 6. Electric Guitar Considerations

### The Electric Path (alternate track)

If the learner selects "Electric guitar" during onboarding, the curriculum makes these adjustments:

**Power Chords Introduced at Level 2 (Session 6) instead of Level 8:**

Power chords (E5, A5, D5) are the bread and butter of electric guitar. They're played with just 2 fingers and are moveable up and down the neck.

- E5: Open low E string + index finger on 2nd fret A string
- A5: Open A string + index finger on 2nd fret D string
- D5: Open D string + index finger on 2nd fret G string

**Why early for electric?**
- Electric strings are thinner, lower tension вҖ” easier to press
- Distortion/overdrive makes power chords sound MASSIVE вҖ” instant rock star feeling
- Power chords unlock punk, metal, and rock songs immediately
- The moveable power chord shape (root + 5th, barred) teaches fretboard navigation early

**Additional electric-path songs:**
- "Smoke on the Water" вҖ” Deep Purple (single-note riff, then power chords)
- "Iron Man" вҖ” Black Sabbath (power chord riff)
- "Smells Like Teen Spirit" вҖ” Nirvana (power chord progression F5-Bb5-Ab5-Db5)
- "Seven Nation Army" вҖ” White Stripes (single-note riff)
- "Back in Black" вҖ” AC/DC (E5, A5)
- "American Idiot" вҖ” Green Day (power chords)
- "Blitzkrieg Bop" вҖ” Ramones (power chords, all downstrokes)

**Tone/amp considerations:**
- Start with clean tone for chord learning (hear mistakes clearly)
- Introduce light overdrive at Level 3 (reward for chord mastery)
- Introduce distortion at Level 4 (power chord level-up)
- Palm muting technique taught earlier (Session 12 instead of 19)

**String muting is different on electric:**
- Unwanted string noise is more audible with amplification
- Teach fretting-hand muting: lightly touch strings you don't want to ring
- Teach picking-hand muting: rest palm near bridge

### Shared Path Elements
Both acoustic and electric paths share:
- All open chord shapes (Em, E, Am, A, D, G, C)
- All strumming patterns
- Session architecture and timing
- Gamification layer
- The electric path simply ADDS power chords and adjusts song selections

## 7. Chord Diagram Specifications

### Diagram Format (for app rendering)

Each chord diagram should render as a standard guitar chord box:

```
Diagram components:
- 6 vertical lines = strings (low E on left, high E on right)
- Horizontal lines = frets (nut at top, frets below)
- Filled circles = finger positions (numbered 1-4)
- X above string = muted/not played
- O above string = open string (played, not fretted)
- Fret number label if chord starts above fret 1
```

### Rendering Specifications for the App

For each chord diagram, the app should store:
```json
{
  "name": "Em",
  "displayName": "E minor",
  "level": 1,
  "strings": [0, 2, 2, 0, 0, 0],
  "fingers": [0, 2, 3, 0, 0, 0],
  "muted": [false, false, false, false, false, false],
  "barreStart": null,
  "barreEnd": null,
  "barreFret": null,
  "startFret": 1,
  "color": "#4CAF50",
  "fingerCount": 2,
  "anchorFingerFrom": null,
  "anchorFingerTo": "E",
  "anchorFinger": 2,
  "anchorFret": 2,
  "anchorString": 5
}
```

Where:
- `strings`: fret number for each string (0 = open, -1 = muted)
- `fingers`: finger number (1=index, 2=middle, 3=ring, 4=pinky, 0=open/muted)
- `muted`: which strings are not played
- `barre*`: barre chord info if applicable
- `color`: the level color for visual consistency
- `anchorFinger*`: which finger stays when transitioning to the next chord

The visual renderer should:
1. Draw 6 vertical strings, 4 horizontal frets
2. Place filled circles at finger positions, numbered
3. Show X (muted) or O (open) above each string
4. Highlight the anchor finger in GOLD when showing transitions
5. Animate finger movement during transition demonstrations
6. Optionally overlay a photo-realistic hand for visual learners
